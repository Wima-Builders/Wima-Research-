Section 05 — Creative Direction

WHAT THE ADS
LOOK LIKE.

Researched across Ghana home services campaigns 2025–2026. The format that wins consistently is authentic smartphone video, shot in natural light, showing the problem in the first 2 seconds. No design agency required.

✓ Do thisShow the problem first. Bare concrete ceiling, open-plan space, unclad block wall, or an ECG bill. If viewers see the problem in the first 2 seconds, they stop scrolling. The contrast between before and after is the content.
✗ Never do thisLead with the finished product. A beautiful ceiling is pleasant. A bare ugly ceiling followed by a beautiful ceiling is compelling. The brain registers before/after contrast within 250 milliseconds.
✓ Do thisShow GHS pricing on screen. "From GHS 85/m²" in the video builds trust, pre-qualifies leads, and eliminates time-wasters. Price transparency is a competitive advantage — most Ghana competitors hide prices.
✗ Never do thisRun designer text flyers. Heavy graphic posters look like spam in the Ghana Facebook feed. Research consistently shows they generate a fraction of authentic smartphone video engagement in construction categories.
✓ Do thisAdd captions to every video. 85% of Facebook and Instagram users in Ghana watch videos without sound. CapCut (free app) adds AI-generated captions automatically in under 2 minutes.
✗ Never do thisTouch campaigns before Day 7. Meta's algorithm needs 7 full days to exit its learning phase. Editing any ad set before Day 7 resets learning, inflates CPA across all four campaigns, and wastes accumulated data.
✓ Do thisShoot vertically — 9:16 ratio. Facebook Reels, Instagram Reels, and Stories are all vertical. A horizontal video fills only 56% of the mobile screen. Shoot on a smartphone held vertically. No tripod needed.
✗ Never do thisBoost posts. The Boost Post button costs more, delivers less targeting precision, cannot build retargeting audiences, and cannot track WhatsApp conversions. Always use Meta Ads Manager. Always.
