Section 01 Strategic Foundation

Campaign Objectives.

Three objectives, sequenced in order of priority. Awareness feeds leads. Leads feed conversions. Every tactical decision in this brief serves one of these three jobs — if it does not, it is cut.

📡 Objective 01 — Awareness
Make Wima Deal Hub known to 50,000+ Accra homeowners before launch day
Position Wima as Ghana's most trusted source for acoustic ceiling, drywall, exterior cladding, and insulation systems. Build brand recognition before asking for a sale. The awareness campaign in Week 2 seeds the retargeting pool that Week 3 converts.
KPI: 50,000 video views · Week 2
💬 Objective 02 — Lead Generation
Generate 15–30 qualified WhatsApp leads at under GHC 75 cost per lead
Drive qualified construction buyers — homeowners, contractors, developers, and diaspora clients — into Wima's WhatsApp pipeline where the BOQ process begins. A qualified lead is defined as: names a product category, states a Ghana location, and responds to the auto-reply.
KPI: 15–30 WhatsApp leads · Weeks 3–4
🏗️ Objective 03 — Conversion
Convert 1–3 qualified leads into confirmed projects generating GHC 10,000+ pipeline
Close the first paying projects within the 28-day window. Even one GHC 5,000 dry wall installation project produces a 3.3× return on the entire GHC 1,500 campaign budget at 6% procurement margin. The conversion happens inside WhatsApp — the campaign drives the door, the BOQ closes the deal.
KPI: 1–3 projects won · Week 4

The Strategic Logic Ghana's construction market is structurally undersupplied and digitally underpenetrated. A $4.77 billion building materials market by 2028 (Statista/GlobalData), a 1.8 million unit housing deficit (Ministry of Works and Housing, January 2026), and Ghana's Prime Building Cost Index at just 2.7% YoY in May 2026 — down from 22% a year earlier, with cement prices falling 14.5% and labour costs contracting 2.0% (GSS, June 2026) — create the most favourable construction launch window since 2021. For Wima, the strategic implication is clear: building finishes (ceilings, partitions, cladding, insulation) are cheaper to install right now than at any point in recent memory, making the BOQ value proposition more compelling to price-sensitive Accra homeowners. Yet most building materials suppliers in Ghana have no digital presence, no WhatsApp pipeline, and no BOQ capability. Wima enters a market with high demand and very low digital competition. The campaign objective is not to create demand — it is to capture demand that already exists but currently has no organised digital channel to flow through.

Section 03 Messaging Framework

What We Say
and How We Say It.

Five messaging pillars, each anchored to a specific audience pain point. Every ad, every post, every WhatsApp reply maps to one of these pillars. Consistency across all touchpoints is what builds trust in a market where most construction suppliers have no coherent voice.

Pillar 01
Transformation is visible. Results are concrete.
Every ad begins with the problem state — the bare concrete ceiling, the open unpartitioned space, the unclad exterior wall. The transformation is the content. Before and after, separated by Wima's work. This pillar owns the visual storytelling across all four categories.
"Concrete ceiling → finished acoustic panel. From GHS 85/m². Free BOQ on WhatsApp."
Bare concrete ceiling in frame 1. White text: "This is your ceiling right now." Time-lapse of installation. Finished ceiling with lighting. Price badge.
Pillar 02
Price transparency builds trust faster than any slogan.
Most Ghana construction suppliers hide prices, creating anxiety and distrust. Wima shows prices on screen in every ad. "From GHS 85/m²" shown in the video converts curious viewers into qualified leads because it pre-qualifies them. A person who messages after seeing the price is ready to buy.
"We show prices. No surprises. Free BOQ delivered within 24 hours."
Price badge appears at the end of every video, every carousel card, every static image. Never hidden. Always specific. Always in GHS.
Pillar 03
Wima is the builder's partner — not just a supplier.
Wima does not just deliver materials. It provides the BOQ, coordinates installation through Jamal and the craftsmen network, arranges financing, and documents the project for diaspora clients. This is a fundamentally different value proposition from any hardware shop or materials vendor in Accra.
"Supply. Install. Document. Finance. One call to Wima covers it all."
Carousel card 3: Wima logo + "Free site visit and BOQ · Install coordination · Bank financing available. WhatsApp us."
Pillar 04
Your Ghana home — completed before you land.
Exclusively for the diaspora audience. The emotional trigger is not the product — it is the fear of returning to an unfinished home, the embarrassment of showing guests a bare-walled property, and the distrust of contractors who disappear. Wima's escrow model and photo documentation remove every one of those fears.
"Pay via Wise or bank transfer. We send photos at every stage. Your Ghana home is safe with us."
Split screen: unfinished Ghana room on the left / finished room on the right. Text: "Building in Ghana from London? We handle it. You watch it happen." WhatsApp CTA.
Pillar 05
Your building is leaking money. Insulation stops it.
Unique to the insulation category. The message frames insulation as a financial saving tool, not a technical product. An ECG electricity bill is the opening image. The copy is about GHC saved per month, not about R-values or thermal conductivity. This is a creative angle that no competitor in Ghana is using for insulation.
"Insulation cuts your cooling bill by up to 30%. At GHS 95/m², it pays for itself within 18 months."
Close-up of ECG bill with amount circled in red. Text: "Your building is leaking money. Every month." Thermal animation. Insulation installed. Price badge. WhatsApp CTA.
